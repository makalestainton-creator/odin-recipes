How to Make Chapati

Description

Chapati is an unleavened flatbread originating from the Indian subcontinent, typically made with whole wheat flour (atta), water, and optional salt or oil, and cooked on a flat skillet (tawa).

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • ¾ to 1 cup warm or hot water (adjust as needed, flour types vary)
  • ¼ tsp salt (optional)
  • tbsp oil or ghee (optional, for softer chapatis)
  • dry flour for dusting
  • Ghee or oil for brushing the cooked chapatis (optional)

Steps

  1. Place 3 cups of flour and add salt in large bowl.
  2. Rub in the ghee, then gradually add the hot water, or enough to form a firm dough.
  3. Mix with a wooden spoon. If the dough is sticky, let it cool for 10 minutes.
  4. If using a stand mixer, use a dough hook and let it mix for 10 minutes.
  5. If kneading by hand, turn the dough on a well-flour surface and knead for 10 minutes until smooth.
  6. Should you notice that the dough is too sticky, add a little flour at a time until a firm dough form. Do not add too much flour.
  7. Place the dough into a bowl and cover with a kitchen cloth or plastic wrap and set aside for one hour.
  8. Divide the dough into 14-16 ports and roll into 15 circles.
  9. Brush the rolled out chapati with melted ghee and form spiralled balls, repeat the process, then cover and let the oiled dough rest for 10 minutes.
  10. You can also roll the dough balls into a circle, cut each circle at the centre then roll tightly to form a cone shape, press each cone shaped ball down on the pointed top.
  11. Reroll the oiled dough balls into a 15 cm (5inches) circle.
  12. Head a thick bottomed pan or tawa on medium heat.
  13. Cook one chapati at a time, dry frying each side until brown bubbles appear, then brush each side with ghee, flattening the surface with a spoon or flipper until both sides are golden brown.
  14. Repeat this process with all chapatis, cooking each side for 15-20 seconds.
  15. Place the in a clean kitchen towel, inside a bowl and cover tightly with a lid. This step will keep your chapatis warm and soft.
  16. Serve with your favourite stew.
